Weird Looking Dogs

Co-directed by the amazing Young Kang and Anya Butler, with music by the stupendous Roy Berardo, Weird Looking Dogs follows the story of two friends catching up while exploring a dungeon, coming across a few adventurous monsters on their way.

With 10:22 of action-packed scenes, this project was a lot to take on as a solo sound designer, foley artist, and mixer, especially because of its lively cartoony feeling with which I have less experience. But, it has become a project which I am extremely proud of, and will go down as one of the hardest projects I completed in my collegiate years. It also holds a special place in my heart because I got to create the voice of the monsters, some of the most memorable characters in the film.

Stead

Stead is an original found-footage film exploring topics like suburbia, family, and marriage. The sound for the film was created from scratch before any work was done on the picture, which made for a fun and unique creative project.

Stead was one of my first ventures with more experimental sound for film, and one of the first live action films I have been a part of producing. The film premiered at my Graduation Recital: Hoj Poj.

All footage used was taken from the Prelinger Archive of Home Movies.

Aki

From the amazing mind of Wanci Hua, with music from the lovely Jason Fujita, Comes a cute, meaningful, tear-jerker about a young fish just trying his best to make a good ramen dish.

Aki offered a few challenged when it came to dialogue editing, since some actors were recorded by myself in a studio, while others sent in recordings from remote home studios, often varying in quality. In the end, I was able to work through the recording differences to produce a seamless dialogue-driven story.

Aki was one of a handful of student films selected to be screened at the 2022 CalArts Character Animation Producers' Show.
